# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

OctoAcme's project management processes provide a comprehensive and structured approach to delivering successful projects while promoting transparency and continuous improvement. The core lifecycle includes initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective stages, each supported by tailored documentation and checklists. At initiation, projects start with a one-pager that captures the business case, stakeholders, goals, timeline, and key risks. This ensures all efforts are grounded in clear, measurable objectives and that stakeholders are properly aligned before significant work begins.

A variety of roles and personas collaborate throughout the process, including Project Managers (PMs), Product Managers (PdMs), Developers, QA/Testing, and stakeholders. Each role has well-defined responsibilities: PMs coordinate delivery and risk management, PdMs optimize for customer value and prioritization, Developers build and test features, and QA ensures quality and acceptance criteria. These clearly delineated roles promote accountability and smooth handoffs at every stage.

Communication is fundamental to the OctoAcme approach. Regular cadences include daily or twice-weekly standups for delivery teams, weekly PM-PdM syncs, monthly stakeholder updates, and ad-hoc escalations as needed. Comprehensive documentation—such as risk registers, status templates, and release notes—ensures all parties have access to up-to-date information, and that issues can be quickly triaged and resolved. Incident communication and escalation paths are documented for both typical risks and security incidents.

Quality and continuous improvement are embedded in every phase. Automated testing, integration and smoke tests, security scanning, and manual QA all serve to ensure feature quality and stability. Regular retrospectives after each sprint, release, or milestone encourage the team to reflect, celebrate successes, and capture actionable lessons for the next cycle. This framework accelerates onboarding, reduces reliance on single individuals, and enables repeatable, reliable project outcomes for the entire team.
